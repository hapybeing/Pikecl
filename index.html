About
FIG 01 — Cortical graph schema. Nodes represent region-of-interest clusters; edges weighted by structural connectivity (DTI).

I study the emergent geometry of thought — how billions of neurons orchestrate perception, memory, and consciousness through patterns of electrical activity spanning milliseconds to years.

My lab at MIT develops computational frameworks that bridge scales: from the microscopic architecture of cortical columns to the macroscopic dynamics of whole-brain networks. We build tools that translate raw neural data into interpretable maps of cognition.
A core belief drives my work — that consciousness is not a property of neurons alone, but a structure that arises from their choreography. Understanding that structure, quantitatively and precisely, is the deepest problem in science.
Before MIT, I completed my doctoral research at the Max Planck Institute for Brain Research under Prof. Wolf Singer, and postdoctoral training at the Salk Institute with Prof. Thomas Sejnowski. My work has been funded by NIH, DARPA, and the Kavli Foundation.

Research Areas

Four Questions
at the Edge of Understanding

Each domain attacks a different facet of the same fundamental question: how does the brain construct subjective experience from electrical noise?

01
01 / 04

Connectome Mapping & Structural Connectivity

We reconstruct whole-brain structural networks at submillimetre resolution using ultra-high-field diffusion MRI and probabilistic tractography. Our connectome atlases reveal how white matter pathways constrain the information-processing hierarchy — from primary sensory cortex to prefrontal association areas. We are currently building the first cell-type-resolved cortical graph of the human temporal lobe.

7T MRI DTI Graph Theory Tractography
02
02 / 04

Neural Oscillations & Conscious States

Brain rhythms — from slow delta waves during sleep to fast gamma oscillations during focused attention — orchestrate neural communication across cortical areas. We use HD-EEG and invasive electrocorticography to measure how phase relationships between oscillatory bands encode the content and clarity of conscious experience. Our theta-gamma coupling model of working memory capacity is now a widely cited framework in the field.

HD-EEG ECoG Phase-Amplitude Coupling LFP
03
03 / 04

Brain–Computer Interfaces & Neural Decoding

Bidirectional BCIs translate neural population codes into motor commands and deliver precisely targeted stimulation to restore lost function. We develop closed-loop systems that adapt in real time to the user's neural state — calibrating stimulation parameters every 50ms using on-device machine learning. In non-human primate models, our system restored naturalistic grasp movements with 94% decoded accuracy following simulated spinal injury.

Utah Array Population Decoding Closed-loop Deep Learning
04
04 / 04

Predictive Neural Coding & Cortical Hierarchy

The predictive brain framework posits that cortex continuously generates forward models of the world and only propagates prediction errors upward. We test this framework using adversarial visual stimuli that selectively violate learned predictions, measuring how mismatch signals propagate through the cortical hierarchy via laminar fMRI. Our recent work proposes that the depth of this hierarchy — not its absolute size — is the critical variable distinguishing human cognition from other primates.

Methods & Techniques

The Tools
of Inquiry

Rigorous methodology is inseparable from rigorous science. Every technique we deploy is chosen for its signal fidelity, temporal or spatial resolution, and interpretive precision.

FIG 02 — Multi-modal recording schema. Concentric rings represent spatial resolution scales from EEG (outer) to single-unit (core).
01

Ultra-High-Field MRI (7T)

Sub-millimetre structural and functional imaging. Enables laminar-resolution BOLD signal, resolving signal differences between cortical layers — critical for testing feedforward vs. feedback predictive coding models.

02

High-Density EEG (256-channel)

Millisecond-resolution electrophysiology with full scalp coverage. Used to characterise oscillatory dynamics, event-related potentials, and phase coupling across cortical networks. Processed with custom source localisation pipelines.

03

Diffusion Tensor Imaging

White matter tractography at 1.05mm isotropic resolution using multi-shell, multi-band protocols. Outputs feed our probabilistic graph-theoretic connectome models. Validated against postmortem histology in collaborating anatomy labs.

04

Electrocorticography (ECoG)

Conducted in partnership with surgical epilepsy programmes. High-gamma broadband power from subdural arrays provides cortical mapping with spatial resolution unavailable from scalp EEG, used to validate intracranial coherence networks.

05

Multi-unit Electrophysiology

96-channel Utah arrays implanted in non-human primate premotor and motor cortex. Used for population vector decoding, dimensionality reduction (LFADS, GPFA), and closed-loop BCI validation at millisecond latency.

06

Computational Modelling

Recurrent neural network models trained to reproduce experimental observations. Dynamic causal modelling (DCM) for effective connectivity. Manifold learning (UMAP, t-SNE) for neural state space visualisation. All code is open-source on GitHub.
